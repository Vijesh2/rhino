The Five Final Stories

Crypto begins with the cleanest position because his errors are distributed rather than concentrated in one catastrophic dependency. Liverpool in first is already expensive, but Liverpool can still climb to fourth, and Crypto benefits if Bournemouth stay sixth while Leeds avoid drifting too far from 16th. A clean Crypto path is Liverpool beating Brentford while Manchester City beat Aston Villa: Liverpool jump Villa into fourth, cutting the damage on Crypto’s title pick from four places to three. Bournemouth then need only avoid defeat at Nottingham Forest, because Brighton start three points behind and would need both a win over Manchester United and a swing in goal difference to steal sixth. At the bottom edge, a Leeds draw or win at West Ham keeps them in the 12th-to-14th band rather than sliding to 15th, so Crypto’s 16th-place Leeds call stays merely bruised instead of becoming another heavy miss.

HeMan wants order at the very bottom and a kinder day for the predicted top six. HeMan has West Ham exactly right in 18th and Burnley almost perfect in 20th. The threat is that the mid-table outsiders keep shifting while Newcastle, HeMan’s sixth-place pick, remains stranded too far down. If Newcastle fail to win at Fulham while Bournemouth avoid defeat at Forest, HeMan’s sixth-place slot stays out of reach. If Brighton beat Manchester United as well, the gap widens: Bournemouth and Brighton crowd the top-six edge, Fulham can jump Newcastle, and HeMan is left paying for a sixth-place prediction on a team still stuck around 12th or 13th.

Bapuji is still alive because the two-point deficit is small and some rivals share the same damaged assumptions. But Bapuji’s Tottenham-at-six pick is an anchor. To win, Bapuji needs enough gains elsewhere that the Spurs damage becomes survivable rather than fatal. One clean route is the switchboard’s Bapuji preset: Manchester United win at Brighton, then Burnley, Palace, Fulham, Liverpool, Manchester City, Forest, Sunderland, Spurs and West Ham all win at home. That gives Bapuji Liverpool fourth and Villa fifth, keeps West Ham close to his 16th-place call, pulls Wolves closer to 18th, and pushes Brentford down towards the bottom-five danger zone he predicted. The Spurs pick still hurts, but the Liverpool/Villa/West Ham/Wolves/Brentford gains are enough to carry it.

Nige is the dramatic one. Sunderland fifth looked wild; now Sunderland are 10th, and even a win can only pull them into the upper mess rather than the Champions League places. Nige’s route is therefore not about perfection. It is about making everybody else bleed at once. In the Nige preset, Sunderland beat Chelsea, Leeds win at West Ham, and Fulham draw with Newcastle while most of the top-half favourites hold serve. That combination drags Chelsea down, lifts Sunderland nearer Nige’s ambitious call, damages Crypto and HeMan on their Sunderland bottom-five picks, and hurts Bapuji by keeping Brentford and West Ham in awkward territory. A second route is similar: Sunderland win, Bournemouth lose at Forest, and Brighton fail to beat Manchester United. That narrows the sixth-to-tenth cluster, making Nige’s strange Sunderland optimism less costly while the leaders lose points on Bournemouth, Chelsea and Sunderland at the same time.

Rocket has the neatest single prediction in the whole game: Arsenal first. But this scoring system rewards the full eleven, not one jewel. With Tottenham and several bottom-five calls dragging, Rocket can still shape who wins, but the model gives him no route to be the winner.
