Bapuji: Champion By Surviving The Ugly Bits

Bapuji’s winning score is funny because it does not look elegant up close. Tottenham in sixth finished 17th, an 11-point crater. Brentford in 19th finished ninth, another 10-point hit. That is not a champion’s prediction card; that is a champion dragging two anvils over the line.

But the final day gave Bapuji just enough of the right kind of ugliness. Aston Villa beating Manchester City kept Villa fourth and made Bapuji’s Villa-fifth call cheap. Liverpool drawing left them fifth, only one off Bapuji’s fourth-place pick. West Ham’s 3-0 win kept them 18th, close enough to Bapuji’s 16th. Wolves stayed 20th, only two away from his 18th. Burnley stayed 19th, one away from his 20th.

The trick was not being brilliant everywhere. It was being less wrong in enough places while everyone else’s Sunderland and Chelsea assumptions detonated.

Crypto: The Favourite Who Got Sunderlanded

Crypto came in with the widest path and still lost by one. That is the cruel version of second place: not a collapse, just a slow leak that becomes visible only after the final whistle.

The Liverpool damage stayed expensive: first predicted, fifth delivered. Chelsea falling to 10th cost Crypto six points. Brentford ninth from an 18th-place pick was a nine-point tax. But the real spicy wound was Sunderland. Crypto had Sunderland 19th; Sunderland beat Chelsea 2-1 and finished seventh. That single pick cost 12 points and turned a likely winner into the person staring at the spreadsheet muttering about newly promoted teams having no respect for narrative structure.

HeMan: Sensible Picks, Senseless League

HeMan did a lot right. Manchester City second: exact. Aston Villa fourth: exact. West Ham 18th: exact. Those are proper, grown-up prediction hits.

Unfortunately, this competition did not reward adulthood. Chelsea third became Chelsea 10th. Newcastle sixth became Newcastle 12th after losing 2-0 at Fulham. Brentford 17th became Brentford ninth. Sunderland 19th became Sunderland seventh. HeMan built a neat house and the final day arrived with a leaf blower.

Third place feels harsh, but fair. HeMan had some of the cleanest individual calls, just not enough insulation against the mid-table nonsense.

Nige: The Chaos Merchant Was Half Right

Nige deserves a small apology from the room. Sunderland fifth looked outrageous before the season and still finished two places higher than anyone sensible expected: seventh, after beating Chelsea on the final day. That was not enough to make the pick good, but it made it less ridiculous than everyone wanted it to be.

The problem is that Nige’s other wild swings charged interest. Bournemouth 16th became Bournemouth sixth: 10 points gone. Brentford 18th became ninth: nine more. Chelsea third became 10th: seven more. Fulham 17th became 11th. Nige correctly smelled chaos, then somehow stood downwind of too much of it.

Rocket: One Perfect Jewel In A Bag Of Gravel

Rocket nailed Arsenal first. Put that on a plaque. Frame it. Then hide the rest of the sheet before guests arrive.

Tottenham sixth finished 17th. Sunderland 19th finished seventh. Brentford 18th finished ninth. Chelsea fourth finished 10th. Those four misses alone are enough to turn a brave card into a cautionary tale. Rocket did not lose because the top was impossible; Rocket lost because the bottom and middle became a blender.
